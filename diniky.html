Criterium for truth. Then comes the second stage in the process of cognition, the stage leading from consciousness back to matter, from ideas back to existence, in which the knowledge gained in the first stage is applied in social practice to ascertain whether the theories, policies, plans or measures meet with the anticipated success. Generally speaking, those that succeed are correct and those that fail are incorrect, and this is especially true of man’s struggle with nature . Furthermore, Man’s knowledge makes another leap through the test of practice. This leap is more important than the previous one. For it is this leap alone that can prove the correctness or incorrectness of the first leap in cognition, i.e., of the ideas, theories, policies, plans or measures formulated in the course of reflecting the objective external world. There is no other way of testing truth. ( marxist.org) This view constrast drastically to what some philosophers defend. Take Spinoza, according to whom truth is its own measure, that is possessing the truth is fully enough to be ascertain of its correcteness. Such truth, he said in his ethics, is reflected by such ideas as the the idea that the sum of the angles on triangle is equal two rights. First, nowadays we know this is no longer true in general since on spaces with positive curvature, the angles add up stricly greater than two rights, first point. The second point is that, Spinoza implicitly defend the existence of some absolute reality that is not changing with times, for example those ideas that he consider from God, i.e. true and eternal. Where are those ideas? Spinoza replies, those are the true ideas! How to find true ideas? In God. In Nature. Spinoza was able to get rid of the anthropomorphic God, yet he did not push his ideas further by defeating totally idealism and embracing fully materialism. But, that is quite making sense since he lived in a era that was still under strong domination by Religions.
So, the truth of ideas, theories, plans, policies can only be known through the test of the practice, and by it alone ( Praxis .) It can be applied to mathematics.
