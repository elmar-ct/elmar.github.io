Continuous Maps

Roughly, a continuous map is a mapping that is open preserving, that is, a function \(f: X\rightarrow Y\) where \(X, Y\) are top spaces, such that the preimage of any open in \(Y\) is open in \(X\).

In order for \(f\) to be continuous, it is enough that the preimages of sets comprising some prebase of \(Y\) be open.
